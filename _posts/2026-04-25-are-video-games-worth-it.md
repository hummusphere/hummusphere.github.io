<h3 class="blog-sub-header"> Let's be real ... </h3>
 
<p> Are games worth it? I have been asking myself this question for a very long time. I have switched the "games" page on this website to <a class="blog-link" href="/projects/">"projects"</a> because I honestly don't think they are worth my time. Now, before you get mad at me, let me explain. </p>

<h3 class="blog-sub-header"> The issue: designed to be addictive? </h3> 

<p> In my prime (or my lowest?) I was a pretty hardcore gamer. I racked up 500 hours on Hearts of Iron IV in around 6 months. I'm ashamed to admit that I have probably spent thousands of hours on Minecraft, Roblox, Clash Royale, and Brawl Stars, and I have nothing to show for it. Well, was it at least fun? Not really. Brawl Stars and Clash Royale are designed to keep you hooked. You receive daily rewards for playing. You are punished for not playing because you fall behind other players. Eventually, you're just logging on and playing not because you want to but because you don't want to fall behind other players. It's not just Supercell games. A lot of Minecraft is just collecting materials for hours. I would just be thinking, "What am I doing with my life?" as I mind-numbingly chopped down trees or mined stone. I wasn't having fun, just playing. Games are addictive because they make progress so easy. It's easier to progress in a game than it is in real life. To keep that feeling of progressing, we keep playing even when we don't really want to. Developers hijack this natural human instinct and use it to make games more addictive. After a while, the game becomes a chore and not what it was supposed to be: fun. Then, you get bored, start a new game, and the cycle repeats. </p>
  
<h3 class="blog-sub-header"> Other side effects </h3> 
  
<p> You are the result of what you consume. If you consume a lot of video games, they will begin to consume your life. This is a side effect that I think most people don't notice. Playing video games hijacks your thoughts. You begin thinking about the game even when you're not playing it. I know I used to do this a lot. I would think about things I wanted to build in Minecraft or strategies for Hearts of Iron IV. It takes away your brain power and leaves you less clarity to actually think about the things that matter. After playing for a long time, you feel like a zombie. </p> 
  
<h3 class="blog-sub-header"> My final verdict </h3> 
  
<p> I'm not going to say that all video games are bad. I did have some good times playing them. What I will say is that they need to be played in moderation. Playing 8 hours a day isn't healthy. 1 to 2 hours a day is probably the sweet spot. Play games that respect your time and aren't designed to be addictive. If you have trouble moderating the amount of time you play games, then it is okay to quit. Video game addiction is a real thing. Personally, I found it hard to moderate, so I just quit entirely. I just want to move on from that point in my life where I wasted a lot of time just playing games. That is why I have changed the "games" tab to "projects."</p> 
  
<p> Thank you for reading. </p>
